# Slang Entries

## Vanakkam anna
phrase: Vanakkam anna
tamil_script: வணக்கம் அண்ணா
literal_meaning: Hello brother
cultural_meaning: A respectful greeting used by locals, especially when addressing someone older or in a position of respect. "Anna" (brother) is used regardless of actual family relation.
common_contexts: ["greeting", "showing respect", "starting conversation"]
speaker_types: ["shopkeeper", "auto_driver", "elderly_local"]
locations: ["temple_streets", "markets", "public_areas"]
visitor_advice: Respond with "Vanakkam" and a slight nod. This shows respect for local customs.

## Periya kovil
phrase: Periya kovil
tamil_script: பெரிய கோவில்
literal_meaning: Big temple
cultural_meaning: The local way to refer to Brihadeeswarar Temple. Locals rarely use the full Sanskrit name and prefer this simple, affectionate term.
common_contexts: ["giving directions", "temple reference", "local pride"]
speaker_types: ["auto_driver", "temple_staff", "elderly_local"]
locations: ["temple_streets", "public_areas"]
visitor_advice: When asking for directions, use "Periya kovil" instead of the full temple name - locals will understand immediately.

## Kadai irukka
phrase: Kadai irukka
tamil_script: கடை இருக்கா
literal_meaning: Is the shop there/open
cultural_meaning: A common way shopkeepers and customers check if a business is operating. Often used when looking for specific items or services.
common_contexts: ["shopping", "business hours", "availability check"]
speaker_types: ["shopkeeper", "elderly_local"]
locations: ["markets", "temple_streets"]
visitor_advice: If someone asks this, they're checking if a shop is open. A simple "yes" or "no" with pointing is helpful.

## Auto la variya
phrase: Auto la variya
tamil_script: ஆட்டோ ல வரியா
literal_meaning: Will you come in auto
cultural_meaning: Auto drivers' way of asking if you want a ride. The tone and context determine if it's a genuine offer or just checking availability.
common_contexts: ["transportation", "fare negotiation", "travel planning"]
speaker_types: ["auto_driver"]
locations: ["temple_streets", "markets", "public_areas"]
visitor_advice: If interested, ask "Evlo?" (how much) to negotiate the fare. Always agree on price before getting in.

## Nalla irukku
phrase: Nalla irukku
tamil_script: நல்லா இருக்கு
literal_meaning: It's good/nice
cultural_meaning: A versatile expression of approval used for everything from food to weather to temple experiences. Shows satisfaction and appreciation.
common_contexts: ["expressing satisfaction", "complimenting", "positive feedback"]
speaker_types: ["shopkeeper", "temple_staff", "elderly_local"]
locations: ["temple_streets", "markets", "public_areas"]
visitor_advice: Use this phrase to show appreciation for local hospitality, food, or experiences. Locals appreciate when visitors use Tamil expressions.

## Konjam wait pannunga
phrase: Konjam wait pannunga
tamil_script: கொஞ்சம் வெயிட் பண்ணுங்க
literal_meaning: Please wait a little
cultural_meaning: A polite way to ask for patience, commonly used in shops, temples, and by service providers. Shows respect while requesting time.
common_contexts: ["service delay", "temple procedures", "shop assistance"]
speaker_types: ["shopkeeper", "temple_staff"]
locations: ["temple_streets", "markets"]
visitor_advice: Be patient when you hear this. Temple and shop procedures often take time, and rushing is considered disrespectful.
